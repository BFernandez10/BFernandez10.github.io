What it is

This project produced a practical manual and workflow for characterizing tri-axial MEMS gyroscopes for space-navigation use, with an emphasis on small-satellite constraints. The guide connects test data to quantitative metrics, modeling, and verification-ready interpretation.

Motivation

MEMS gyros are attractive for small satellites, but their performance can vary significantly across devices, axes, temperature, and test conditions. A repeatable methodology improves consistency across campaigns and enables more grounded expectations for attitude estimation performance.

My contribution

  • Defined a repeatable workflow for computing Allan variance from mission test datasets.
  • Translated Allan variance outputs into mathematical models to predict bias behavior over time.
  • Assessed axis-dependent non-linearities and documented how they influence model accuracy.
  • Packaged the result as a manual for internal use and future test standardization.

Results

  • Manual and modeling workflow made available to the ITA Space Center community.
  • Improved traceability and repeatability for gyro verification and test planning.
  • Provided a clearer basis for navigation-oriented sensor acceptance criteria.
