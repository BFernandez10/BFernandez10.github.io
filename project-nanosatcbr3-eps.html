What it is

This project developed an Electrical Power Subsystem (EPS) concept plan for the NANOSATC-BR3 nanosatellite, leveraging literature, best practices, and heritage insights from NANOSATC-BR1. The concept focuses on early-stage design clarity, traceability, and interface awareness.

Motivation

EPS decisions made early can constrain the whole spacecraft architecture. A clear concept definition helps reduce rework by documenting assumptions, sizing logic, interfaces, and risk areas before detailed design accelerates.

My contribution

  • Structured the EPS concept plan using literature and mission heritage references.
  • Documented key assumptions, interfaces, and early sizing direction for the subsystem.
  • Supported traceability of decisions for later design phases and stakeholder communication.

Results

  • Delivered a concept definition that supports early design decisions and justification.
  • Improved clarity on subsystem architecture, constraints, and future verification needs.
