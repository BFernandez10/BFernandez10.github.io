Projects

ITASAT-2 Hardware-in-the-Loop Roadmap for AOCS Verification

Led the definition of a hardware-in-the-loop (HIL) implementation plan for the ITASAT-2 microsatellite at the ITA Space Center (CEI) through the E2MoC Lab. The work structured the end-to-end steps required to bring COTS components into a realistic Attitude and Orbit Control Subsystem (AOCS) verification flow, aligning simulation fidelity with practical embedded constraints.

Managed the project using a hybrid PMBOK approach, producing a Work Breakdown Structure (WBS), work packages, and a Gantt schedule within a multidisciplinary and international team. Built the foundation of an attitude and orbit control simulator in MATLAB using mission-representative data, and specified STM development boards to emulate a flat-sat architecture for closed-loop testing.

The project is ongoing, prioritizing both simulation verisimilitude and physical emulation to generate verification-ready deliverables for mission use.

MEMS Gyroscope Characterization Manual for Small-Satellite Navigation

Authored a practical manual for characterizing tri-axial MEMS gyroscopes for space-navigation applications, tailored to small-satellite constraints. Using test data from the SPORT mission, I defined a repeatable workflow for computing Allan variance and translating results into mathematical models that predict sensor bias behavior.

The work also assessed axis-dependent non-linearities and their impact on model accuracy, enabling more grounded expectations for attitude estimation performance during design and test campaigns.

The manual is available to the ITA Space Center community and supports consistent, traceable gyro verification for future missions.

Electrical Power Subsystem Concept Definition for NANOSATC-BR3

Developed the Electrical Power Subsystem (EPS) concept for the NANOSATC-BR3 nanosatellite, the third mission in the NANOSATC-BR family. The work produced a structured concept plan grounded in literature and heritage practices from NANOSATC-BR1, supporting early design decisions and traceability.

This contribution strengthened my systems-engineering foundation in spacecraft power architecture, requirements thinking, and design justification under real mission constraints.
